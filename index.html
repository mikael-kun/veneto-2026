Thursday — Doge's Palace, then food, wine & antiques
A slow start on the Piazza, then a north-to-south amble through the bàcari and antique dealers.
10:00
Palazzo Ducale · San Marco
The Doge's Palace, done empty-handed before any shopping (bags go through security). Combined St Mark's Square Museums ticket, €30/adult — codes 96PWIDWO35 & X8HZD6UUMY. Tickets (PDF)
11:30
Museo Correr · San Marco
Across the Piazza, on the same combined ticket — imperial rooms and Venetian painting. A relaxed hour before lunch. Codes 1K3X3WUHMW & 9N3OCUWEHT. Tickets (PDF)
~12:45
Cantine del Vino già Schiavi · Dorsoduro
Lunch, ~15–20 min west over the Accademia bridge. Legendary canal-side bàcaro — inventive cicchetti and great-value wine by the glass. Kitchen closes at 2pm, so don't dawdle.
~13:45
Osteria Al Squero · Dorsoduro
150 m on — cicchetti and a spritz facing the squero, where gondolas are still built and repaired by hand.
~14:45
Claudia Canestrelli · Dorsoduro
By the Guggenheim — jewellery made from antique fragments, Murano glass and pearls. Closes 3:30pm, go right after lunch.
~16:00
San Marco antique quarter · San Marco
Back over the Accademia bridge to the dealer-packed lanes around Santo Stefano & San Maurizio — Maurizio Lorenzo for Art Nouveau. The carrying starts here, with the palace well behind you.
~18:30
Vino Vero · Cannaregio
Drift back to the Fondamenta della Misericordia strip for golden-hour wine & cicchetti — 5 min from P 1779.
Doing the palace first means anything you buy later just rides along to aperitivo — no surrendering fragile antiques to a museum cloakroom. The early Antichità Al Ghetto stop in Cannaregio drops out of this version (pop in on the 8th evening if you'd like it), and the Campo San Maurizio market runs only ~5 weekends a year, so it won't be on this Thursday. If the afternoon turns hot, the Zattere promenade catches the breeze.

Lunch east in Castello, the Biennale next door, then west to the concert.

~19:30
Teatro La Fenice · San Marco
20:00 — Cornelius Meister conducts Strauss Don Juan, Dvořák The Noon Witch & Brahms Symphony No. 2. Platea A, Fila N, Posti 1 & 2. ~20 min on foot, or vaporetto line 1, from the Arsenale. Dress smart-casual; arrive ~30 min before. Tickets (PDF)
The day is a wide “V” — P 1779 (Cannaregio) sits north of both the Arsenale (east) and La Fenice (central). Doubling back to refresh at the B&B costs ~35 min each way, so it's smoother to head straight from the Arsenale toward San Marco and freshen up over an early aperitivo near the theatre.
Sunday's tasting — Monte Fasolo, 16:30. “The Grape Route” at the Enoteca Monte Fasolo, 2 guests, €25pp, booked under Mira (Laukkanen). Pending — watch for the confirmation email. Keep the day relaxed: Arquà Petrarca and the pool earlier, then the short drive over. Mikael's driving back afterwards, so he'll want to go easy and lean on the spittoon — Mira can enjoy more freely.
Monday's tasting — Vignalta, 14:30. The “Terroir Experience” at Vignalta in Arquà Petrarca, 2 guests, €25pp, in English — confirmed (booking b54ed233-8ffb-44b2-a072-fcb502ec4442). One of the Colli Euganei's benchmark estates, ~10 min from Le Volpi. Many indoor sights (the Este museum, Villa dei Vescovi) close on Mondays, so pair the tasting with open-air stops — and, as on Sunday, Mikael's at the wheel afterwards.
Padova on the way out (14th). A sentimental loop through Mira's exchange-year city — ~40 min from Le Volpi and roughly on the road to VCE. The historic centre is compact and walkable: Caffè Pedrocchi (the grand “doorless” café of 1831 — the mint-cream caffè Pedrocchi is the thing to order), the market bustle of Piazza della Frutta, the astronomical Torre dell'Orologio on Piazza dei Signori, and a quiet pass by Mira's old place on Via Paolo Fambri. Park outside the ZTL — the big Prato della Valle lot is an easy walk in. Leaving Padova by ~14:15 keeps the airport arrival relaxed.

VI From the Wine Atlas
Plate I · Northeast Italy, mid-1980s
Plate II · Eastern Veneto & Friuli, mid-1980s
The map, forty years on
What's changed between these pages and your glasses this week.
Colli EuganeiA quiet green ring with a Moscato note. Now the hills' flagship is Fior d'Arancio DOCG (2010) — aromatic yellow Moscato, dry to passito — alongside serious Bordeaux-blend reds. Vignalta, your Monday tasting, was founded in 1980, right as this atlas went to print.
TocaiAll over these maps. Renamed Friulano in 2007 after Hungary won the Tokaj name — same grape, and you may still hear locals order “un tocai”.
ProseccoThe atlas calls the still white “fairly nondescript, but excellent material for sparkling wine”. Some understatement — though the Cartizze hill it singles out is still the benchmark.
VeneziaBlank then, almost blank now — but since the 2000s Venissa on Mazzorbo has revived the native Dorona grape in a walled lagoon vineyard, and a few rows grow on Sant'Erasmo.
OmbraThe atlas notes the Venetian word for a glass of wine at a café — literally “shadow”. Still current in every bàcaro on your Cannaregio strip: un'ombra de vin.
Pages from the Finnish edition of Hugh Johnson's World Atlas of Wine — photographed at home before departure, carried here in spirit.
